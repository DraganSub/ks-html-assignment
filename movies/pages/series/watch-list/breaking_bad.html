    Summary:

    "Breaking Bad" is a critically acclaimed television series that aired from 2008 to 2013. The show follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher in Albuquerque, New Mexico, who turns to cooking and selling methamphetamine after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer.

    Faced with financial struggles and wanting to provide for his family, Walter partners with former student Jesse Pinkman to enter the drug trade. As they become deeply entangled in the dangerous and violent world of drug manufacturing and distribution, Walter adopts the alter ego "Heisenberg" and becomes a ruthless and cunning drug kingpin.

    Throughout the series, Walter's transformation from a mild-mannered teacher into a criminal mastermind is central to the narrative. His actions have far-reaching consequences, impacting his family, friends, and enemies.

    "Breaking Bad" is lauded for its riveting storytelling, complex characters, and Bryan Cranston's Emmy-winning performance as Walter White. The show explores themes of morality, the consequences of one's choices, and the fine line between good and evil. "Breaking Bad" remains a landmark in television history and continues to be celebrated as one of the greatest TV series of all time.
