    Summary:

    "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" is a 2011 mystery thriller film directed by David Fincher, based on the novel of the same name by Stieg Larsson.

    The story follows Mikael Blomkvist, a journalist who is hired by wealthy industrialist Henrik Vanger to investigate the decades-old disappearance of his niece, Harriet. As Mikael delves into the Vanger family's dark secrets, he teams up with Lisbeth Salander, a brilliant and enigmatic computer hacker with a troubled past.

    Together, Mikael and Lisbeth uncover a web of deceit, corruption, and violence that goes far beyond Harriet's disappearance. As they get closer to the truth, they put their lives at risk and must confront their own personal demons.

    "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" is known for its intense and suspenseful plot, complex characters, and the captivating performance of Rooney Mara as Lisbeth Salander. The film received critical acclaim for its stylish direction, atmospheric tone, and faithful adaptation of the bestselling novel.
