    Summary:

    "The Babadook" is a horror film released in 2014, written and directed by Jennifer Kent.

    The movie follows Amelia, a single mother struggling to cope with the death of her husband and the challenges of raising her troubled son, Samuel. One night, Samuel chooses a mysterious children's book titled "Mister Babadook" as his bedtime story.

    As Amelia reads the book, they become plagued by a sinister presence, the Babadook, a malevolent entity that preys on their fears and insecurities. The Babadook begins to terrorize them, blurring the line between reality and imagination.

    As Amelia's sanity begins to unravel, she must confront her darkest fears and protect her son from the malevolent force that threatens to consume them.

    "The Babadook" is praised for its psychological horror, atmospheric tension, and powerful performances by Essie Davis as Amelia and Noah Wiseman as Samuel. The film explores themes of grief, motherhood, and the manifestation of inner demons, making it a thought-provoking and chilling addition to the horror genre.
