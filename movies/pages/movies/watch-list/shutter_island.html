    Summary:

    "Shutter Island" is a psychological thriller film released in 2010, directed by Martin Scorsese. The movie is based on the novel of the same name by Dennis Lehane.

    The story follows U.S. Marshal Teddy Daniels, played by Leonardo DiCaprio, and his partner Chuck Aule, portrayed by Mark Ruffalo, who visit Shutter Island, a remote and foreboding mental institution for the criminally insane. Their purpose is to investigate the mysterious disappearance of a patient named Rachel Solando.

    As they delve deeper into the investigation, Teddy begins to experience disturbing visions and encounters strange occurrences on the island. The more Teddy digs into the case, the more he questions the motives of the hospital staff and the true nature of the institution.

    As the plot unfolds, the line between reality and illusion becomes increasingly blurred, leading to a shocking and mind-bending revelation that challenges everything Teddy thought he knew.

    "Shutter Island" is praised for its intense atmosphere, intricate storytelling, and standout performances from its cast. The film's exploration of the human mind and the themes of memory, perception, and identity contribute to its reputation as a thought-provoking and suspenseful thriller.
